LifeSpeak — Minneapolis, Minnesota — June 2025 to present.

I'm Chief of Staff. At a small PE-backed company that title is mostly a
disguise for "the person who builds the operating system the executive team
uses." The actual work splits roughly into three streams.

First, strategic planning. I facilitate planning workshops with the executive
leadership team to define roadmaps for 8 cross-functional PMO initiatives.
That accelerated decision cycles by ~25% and locked in alignment on more than
$1M of planned investments. Workshops sound soft until you actually run them
— most of the leverage is in pre-reads and tracking what was committed.

Second, governance. I manage a pipeline of 12 concurrent projects through a
standardized framework I built in Palantir Foundry and Excel. The framework
exists because the previous "system" was a slide deck nobody read. With it,
on-time delivery moved from 70% to 92%, and every project now has KPIs that
are mapped back to investor expectations and tracked weekly.

Third, Board-level reporting for the PE sponsors. I codified 15 performance
metrics into a live Board Reporting app — the same numbers across the same
cadence, no spreadsheet roulette before each meeting. That work drove a 20%
uplift in operational KPIs (because what gets measured gets shipped) and
surfaced ~$500K in efficiency opportunities the team has actively pulled on.

The thing I like about Chief of Staff is the breadth. The thing that makes it
real is that the engineering instincts I built at FDE work — small useful
tools, clean evaluation, idempotent pipelines — apply directly.
